Coat or Hat? is a fresh look at the weather forecast. Often people only check the weather in order to work out what to wear before they head outside. Coat or Hat? takes a playful and direct approach to this interaction by allowing users to view humanised data instead of scientific. Rather than trying to translate degrees into layers of clothing users can see a visual of what to wear. This will be in the form of three interactions on the mobile friendly website and companion app for those who prefer not go through a browser on their mobile device.

A suggested outfit for the current location is calculated from the scientific forecast data and a summary of user data collected through the new service.

The user also can then see other users entries of what they are wearing spreading out from this. These entries are relevant to the current location the user is in.

And finally the user can also input their choice of outfit for the day, allowing collection of data to enable the fun ability to see what others have chosen to wear in different locations.

This makes the mobile service especially useful to travellers wanting to check out what to wear when they arrive at their destination. Coat or Hat? also offers opportunities for local businesses to advertise their products relevant to what users are wearing on the day. For example a travelling arriving at their destination may see that most people in the area are wearing beanies and nearby shops selling beanies can be suggested.

The user enters their outfit by using a friendly draggable slider to add on layers and layers of clothing. This scale corresponds to temperature data and therefore can be translated into an emotive collection of the relative degrees people feel it is outside. Also there will be option for the user to select a raincoat and other specific weather related accessories.

This allows the user to input information in a way that is aesthetically pleasing and fun to use to encourage data collection. Also needed will be a geolocation API so that the user can be shown other entries relevant to their location and so that the data they submit will have a location logged. To construct the website a framework and plugins would also be used as well as a database to store accounts and other information.

Users will have an accounts so that they can upload their choices and rate others on their accuracy allowing a reputation based form of troll prevention. Everyone enjoys a peek into others’ lives and Coat or Hat? satisfies that need without breaching privacy as all that is shown is the username and city the user is located in.

Technical Requirements:

HTML and CSS will be used to create the website along with jQuery and Javascript.

Skeleton is a framework which I tested to make this concept article and found it satifactory. It's simple column system stacks well for mobile and does not have the styling of bootstrap which will make the unique aesthetic of the new website easy to do. Skeleton Website

App:

The app will be very similar to the website except with additional functions specific to the mobile device.

Notifications can be used to advise the user what others are wearing, to remind them to check what to wear or to remind them to enter their own outfit. They can also be used for the travel aspect to notify the user that they may need to change their outfit upon nearing their destination.

The location finder can be implemented the Google geolocation API
